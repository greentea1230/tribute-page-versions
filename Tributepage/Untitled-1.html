Francis S. Collins, M.D., Ph.D

Dr. Francis Collins is a geneticist-physician and a former director of the US National Insititute of Health. He earned his Ph.D in physical chemistry from Yale University and his M.D. from the Univeristy of North Carolina.

Genetics

He is known for leading the Human Genome Project, which began in 1990 and concluded in 2003, with the primary objective of identifying, mapping, and sequencing the human genome. It was conducted in partnership with numerous universities from around the world including in countries such as the UK, China, France, and Japan.

Dr. Collins assumed leadership of the project in 1993, leading the project to achieve various milestones such as producing the first complete sequence of a human chromosome, producing the first complete sequence of a bacterial genome, and eventually producing the most complete sequencing of the human genome at around 92%.

Medicine

In the field of medicine Dr. Collins's lab has identified key disease genes for various diseases such as cystic fibrosis, type 2 diabetes, and hutchinson-gilford progeria syndrome (HGPS).

His laboratory has given special focus on finding treatment options for the aforementioned HGPS and type-2 diabetes such as gene therapy and pharmaceutical treatment.
